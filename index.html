Our Alternative Timeline

In this alternative timeline, the Union did not collapse in 1991. Instead, a successful and radical reformation led to unprecedented economic and technological prosperity. This resurgence of socialist power resulted in a historic expansion across Eastern Europe and the Balkans.

Nations such as Poland, Czechoslovakia (split into Czechia and Slovakia), Hungary, Romania, Bulgaria, and the states of Yugoslavia chose to integrate into the renewed Union. Today, a total of 30 republics stand united under the red banner, forging a new path for humanity in a multipolar world.
